### VIDEO 1: "The Scale Test"
**Platform:** TikTok + Reels
**Length:** 15-25 seconds
**Hook (text on screen + voiceover):** "Your hoodie weighs this much..." *[drop a thin fast fashion hoodie on a kitchen scale — show ~340g]* "...ours weighs this much." *[drop NEWGARMENTS hoodie — show 800g+]*
**Shots needed:**
- Close-up of kitchen/postal scale at zero
- Hand dropping cheap hoodie on scale (get one from H&M/Zara for comparison)
- Cut to scale showing low weight
- Hand dropping NEWGARMENTS hoodie on scale
- Cut to scale showing heavy weight — hold this shot for 2 seconds
- Quick cut to someone wearing the hoodie, walking away confidently
**Text overlay:** "Stop paying premium for paper-thin hoodies."
**Why it converts:** This is undeniable visual proof. No words needed. The scale doesn't lie. Directly kills the #1 objection (is this just another overpriced thin hoodie?). Cole Buxton proved this format gets 100K-1M+ views consistently.
**CTA:** "Link in bio. No restock."

**Platform:** TikTok + Reels
**Length:** 10-15 seconds
**Hook:** No voiceover. Just ambient sound.
**Shots needed:**
- Hold a cheap hoodie at shoulder height → drop it. It floats down slowly like tissue paper.
- Hold NEWGARMENTS hoodie at same height → drop it. It thuds down with weight and substance.

**Why it converts:** Pure visual. The contrast is visceral. This is the kind of content people send to their group chat with "bro look at this."
**CTA:** None needed — it sells itself. Pin a comment with the link.

### VIDEO 3: "I Cut It Open"
**Platform:** TikTok + Reels
**Length:** 30-45 seconds
**Hook (voiceover):** "I cut open our hoodie so you can see what you're actually paying for."
**Shots needed:**
- Close-up of scissors cutting through the seam of a NEWGARMENTS hoodie
- Show the seam allowance (how much extra fabric is folded into the seam)
- Show the stitching type — double stitched, reinforced at stress points
- Show the inner fleece lining up close — run fingers through it
- Show the fabric thickness from the cut edge — macro shot
- Compare side by side with a cut-open fast fashion hoodie (thin, single stitch, minimal seam allowance)
**Text overlay sequence:**
- "Double stitched seams"
- "Reinforced stress points"
- "450+ GSM French terry"
- "This is what quality looks like inside"
**Why it converts:** Destruction content always gets attention. But this is destruction WITH education. It answers the question "what am I actually getting?" with physical evidence. Gen Z has been burned by products that look good outside but are garbage inside.
**CTA:** "Built different. Link in bio."

### VIDEO 5: "Is NEWGARMENTS a Scam? Let Me Show You Everything"

### VIDEO 11: "When It's Gone, It's Archive"
**Platform:** TikTok + Reels + Stories
**Length:** 20-30 seconds
**Hook (voiceover, calm and factual):** "We made 50 of these. When they're gone, they're archive. No restock. No exceptions."
**Shots needed:**
- Slow overhead shot of all pieces laid out neatly (show the limited quantity visually)

## FILMING TIPS FOR YOUR VIDEOGRAPHER

1. **Lighting:** Natural light or warm studio light. Avoid harsh fluorescent. For product close-ups, soft overhead lighting. For lifestyle, golden hour or moody window light.

2. **Audio:** For ASMR/packing videos, use a separate mic or get very close. The sound of heavyweight fabric is a selling point — make sure it's captured.

3. **Angles:** For product shots, shoot at eye level or slightly below (makes the piece look substantial). For lifestyle, mix eye-level with low angles (makes the person look confident/powerful).

4. **Movement:** Slow, deliberate camera movement for cinematic content. Quick cuts for TikTok energy. Film everything slightly longer than you think you need — you can always cut, you can't add.

5. **The weight rule:** In EVERY shot, the weight and thickness of the fabric should be visible. This is your #1 differentiator. If the hoodie looks thin on camera, adjust the lighting, angle, or handling until the weight reads on screen.

6. **Multiple takes:** Film each setup at least 3 times. Film in both portrait (9:16 for TikTok/Reels/Stories) and landscape (16:9 for website/YouTube) if possible.

7. **Raw over polished:** For TikTok content, slightly raw/imperfect footage performs better than over-produced content. Don't color grade TikTok content to look like a commercial — it triggers the "this is an ad" instinct and they scroll past.

8. **The model matters:** If using a model, they should look like your audience (18-24, streetwear-confident, not a traditional fashion model). Confidence > looks. The audience needs to see themselves.

1. **The Scale Test** (Video 1) — immediate trust builder, shareable

4. **"Is NEWGARMENTS a Scam?"** (Video 5) — addresses the elephant in the room

### Posting cadence:
- TikTok: 4-5x per week (the algorithm rewards frequency)
- Instagram Reels: 3x per week (slightly more polished versions)
- Instagram Stories: daily (behind-the-scenes, polls, countdowns)
- Instagram feed: 2x per week (carousels, hero shots)

### Repurposing:
- Every TikTok can become an Instagram Reel (adjust aspect ratio if needed)
- Every Reel can become 2-3 Stories (cut into segments)

- B-roll becomes Story content
