## WHAT'S GOING WRONG (Honest Assessment)

### Problem 1: Repetitive Captions Killing Engagement

Your last 31 posts use essentially **3 captions on repeat:**

1. "When stock is gone, our website closes... Act fast!" (used 6+ times)
2. "The first 10 comments will get a surprise :)" (used 5+ times)
3. "Archive sale up to 77% OFF + FREE GIFT + FREE SHIPPING" (used 6+ times)

**Why this kills you:**
- The TikTok algorithm detects duplicate/similar content and throttles distribution. When you post the same caption with similar content multiple times in the same day, each subsequent post gets less reach.
- Your followers see the same message and stop engaging. Comments dry up. The algorithm reads low engagement as "not interesting" and stops pushing it.
- **Evidence from your data:** On Feb 16, you posted 10 pieces of content. The first got 529K views. The last got 2,638 views. That's a **99.5% drop** within the same posting session.

### Problem 2: Posting Floods Instead of Consistent Cadence

Your posting pattern:
- Feb 16: **10 posts in one day**
- Feb 17: **5 posts in one day**
- Feb 18: **7 posts in one day**
- Then silence until Mar 4 (3 posts)

**Why this kills you:**
- TikTok's algorithm rewards **consistent daily posting**, not bursts. Posting 10 pieces in one day means each post competes with itself for your followers' attention.
- The algorithm gives each post ~1-2 hours to prove itself before deciding to push or suppress it. If you post 10 in a day, most never get their chance.
- Your Feb 17 posts got 560-696 views each. That's **shadowban territory** for a 77K-follower account. The algorithm literally stopped distributing your content because you flooded it.

### Problem 3: Sale/Discount Messaging Everywhere

Almost every recent post mentions: "76% OFF", "77% OFF", "79% OFF", "Archive sale", "FREE GIFT + FREE SHIPPING"

**Why this kills you (based on your own audience research):**
- Your audience **explicitly rejects** brands that feel "cheap" or discount-driven. Your own research says: "Permanent sales destroy perceived value and trust."
- Your pricing psychology research says: "Sitewide percentage discounts visible to everyone: signals desperation."
- Corteiz, Trapstar, Cole Buxton -- NONE of them run visible discount content on TikTok. They sell out at full price.
- When every post screams "SALE", you look like a clearance store, not a premium streetwear brand.
- **The data confirms it:** Your 1.6M-view post said "Not made for everyone" (exclusivity). Your worst posts all said "77% OFF" (desperation). The audience is telling you what they want.

### Problem 4: No Variety in Content Types

Your recent videos are almost all the same format: product showcase clips with sale messaging. There's no:
- Trust-building content (quality proof, behind-the-scenes, fabric close-ups)
- Identity content (fit checks, styling, NPC vs. main character)
- Educational content (GSM breakdown, what makes heavyweight different)
- Storytelling (founder story, brand origin, why you started)
- Community content (customer unboxings, reviews, real people wearing it)

**Why this kills you:**
- TikTok rewards **content diversity.** The algorithm tracks content categories and pushes creators who serve multiple viewer interests.
- Your audience needs 7-10 trust touchpoints before buying from an unknown brand. If every post is "BUY NOW 76% OFF," you're skipping trust-building entirely and going straight to conversion. That doesn't work with a skeptical Gen Z audience.

### Problem 5: Slideshows Are Underused

Only 7 out of 31 recent posts are slideshows (22%). Yet your slideshows get **4.4x more views** and **10.3x more saves** than your videos.

**The math is simple:** If you posted 5 slideshows per week instead of 2-3, and each averaged even half your slideshow performance (~160K views), you'd be generating 800K views per week from slideshows alone. Currently your videos average 73K views -- you'd need to post 11 videos to match 1 slideshow's reach.

## RECOMMENDATIONS (Priority Order)

### IMMEDIATE FIXES

1. **Stop posting multiple times per day.** Maximum 1-2 posts per day, spaced 4+ hours apart. Your data shows each additional same-day post gets exponentially less reach.

2. **Stop using the same caption on multiple posts.** Every post needs a unique hook. Even if the product is the same, the angle must be different.

3. **Remove all discount % messaging from TikTok content.** Your best-performing content has zero discount mentions. Your worst has heavy discount messaging. Follow the data.

4. **Shift to 60-80% slideshows, 20-40% video.** Your slideshows outperform videos by 4.4x on views and 10.3x on saves. This isn't a suggestion -- it's what your own numbers demand.

### CONTENT STRATEGY SHIFT

5. **Use the slideshow concepts from SLIDESHOW_CONTENT_PLAN.md** -- they're designed for exactly the gaps in your current content (trust, identity, education, smart scarcity).

6. **Rotate through content categories:**
   - Monday: Trust slideshow (quality proof, transparency)
   - Tuesday: Desire slideshow (lookbook, styling)
   - Wednesday: Video (behind-the-scenes, ASMR packing)
   - Thursday: Identity slideshow (NPC vs main character, "where'd you get that")
   - Friday: Scarcity slideshow (limited stock, archive wall)

7. **Replace discount hooks with value hooks:**
   - INSTEAD OF: "79% OFF this outfit"
   - USE: "460 GSM. Your hoodie weighs half this."
   - INSTEAD OF: "Archive sale + FREE GIFT"
   - USE: "50 made. When they're gone, they're archive."

### CAPTION RULES

8. **Every caption must be unique.** Never copy-paste across posts.

9. **Hook formulas to rotate:**
   - Identity: "Not made for everyone" / "For the ones who know" / "Stop dressing like a background character"

   - Scarcity: "50 made. Archive when gone." / "This closes in 24 hours"
   - Social proof: "3 people asked where I got this today"

10. **Stop leading with price/discount.** Lead with identity, quality, or exclusivity. Price comes last, if at all.
