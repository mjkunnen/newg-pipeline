# PRICING PSYCHOLOGY & URGENCY HOOKS FOR NEWGARMENTS

---

## THE #1 RULE: DO NOT DISCOUNT YOUR CORE PRODUCT

Your €45 hoodie at 460 GSM is already exceptional value. The strategy is to **communicate that value**, not reduce the price.

**How top brands handle it:**
- **Corteiz**: Never discounts. Destroyed product rather than mark it down. Items resell 2-5x.
- **Trapstar**: Rarely discounts mainline. "Archive sale" framing only.
- **Cole Buxton**: Prices £100-150, rarely discounted. Restocks are events.

**Pattern**: The brands that win never discount. They use scarcity, exclusivity, and cultural capital.

---

## WHEN DISCOUNTS DESTROY YOUR BRAND

- Frequent sales (more than 1-2/year): customers learn the "real" price
- Sitewide percentage discounts visible to everyone: signals desperation
- Discounting core/hero products: you ARE the 460 GSM hoodie — discount it, discount the brand
- Discounting in first 6-12 months: never established full-price baseline

## GEN Z & PRICE SENSITIVITY

- More price-sensitive than millennials BUT reject brands that feel "cheap"
- Will pay premium when justified (quality, sustainability, community, culture)
- Want to feel **smart**, not like they got a deal
- "I found this brand before it blew up" > "I got 30% off because they were desperate"

---

## DISCOUNT FORMATS RANKED (If You Must)

### SAFE (Low Brand Damage)
1. **Free shipping over €75** — doesn't feel like a discount, encourages 2-item orders
2. **Hidden codes in TikTok Lives** — 10%, valid 24hrs, creates exclusivity
3. **Friend referral credit** — "Give €5, get €5" — feels like community, not discounting
4. **Bundle pricing** — "Hoodie + Tee: €70" (vs €75 separately) — frame as "set"

### USE CAREFULLY
5. **10% welcome discount for email signup** — standard, expected
6. **Student discount (10-15%)** — quietly available, not advertised
7. **Early access pricing** — "€42 first 24hrs, then €47" — rewards being early

### NEVER DO
8. Sitewide percentage off (max once/year, Black Friday only, max 15-20%)
9. Flash sales on core product
10. Permanent outlet/sale section — **destroys brand entirely**

---

## ALTERNATIVES TO DISCOUNTS (Your Primary Strategy)

### Scarcity Playbook
- **Limited runs**: 50-200 units per colorway. Show the number on camera.
- **Time-limited drops**: Announce 24-48hrs before, close after 48-72hrs
- **Archive/Vault**: Sold-out = gone forever. Show the archive page.
- **Never-restock policy**: Core silhouettes return in new colors only

### Exclusivity Playbook
- **Email list early access**: 12-24hr head start before public drop
- **WhatsApp/Discord inner circle**: First access, no discounts needed
- **Friends & Family**: Private perk for repeat customers (3+ orders). Earned, not given.
- **Founding member perks**: First 100 customers = permanent early access

### Value-Add Instead of Price-Cut
- **Free gift with purchase**: Branded tote, sticker pack (costs €2-3, feels like €10-15)
- **Upgraded packaging for first X orders**: Premium collector's box
- **Mystery gift**: "Every order this drop includes a mystery extra"

## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY ON TIKTOK

### Price Anchoring (Your Most Powerful Tool)
Script: *"460 GSM. For context, [brand] charges €120 for 400 GSM. Cole Buxton is £125. We're at €45. Not because it's cheaper — because we don't have the overhead."*

### Cost-Per-Wear Framing
*"€45 ÷ 200 wears = €0.22 per wear. That Zara hoodie for €25 thrown away after 30 wears? €0.83 per wear. We're literally cheaper."*

### Production Cost Breakdown (Gen Z loves transparency)
*"Where your €45 goes: €18 fabric (460 GSM Portuguese cotton). €8 production (cut & sewn, not mass-produced). €4 packaging. €6 shipping & operations. €9 is our margin. We're not making €30 per hoodie like big brands."*

### Price Point Psychology
- **€45 vs €50**: €45 feels significantly cheaper (left-digit effect). €45 is impulsive; €50 requires justification.
- **Never use .99 pricing**: Dead for premium. Gen Z sees it as manipulation.
- **Stay at €45**: Clean, memorable, below the "I need to think" threshold.
- **After 3-4 successful drops**: Test €47-48 to check elasticity.

## URGENCY HOOKS WITHOUT DISCOUNTS

### What Works (Ranked by Conversion Lift)
1. **Real stock scarcity** ("Only 8 left in your size"): +25-35% conversion
2. **Time-limited drops** (hard close date): +20-30% conversion
3. **Social proof** ("X sold today"): +15-25% conversion
4. **Countdown timers**: +10-20% conversion
5. **"Back in stock" alerts**: 40-60% email-to-purchase rate

### TikTok-Specific Urgency
- *"We dropped 200 units two hours ago. We're at 47."*
- Show Shopify dashboard, order notifications, stock levels dropping
- *"This colorway is now archived. If you got one, you got one."*
- Show DMs asking "when's the restock?" for sold-out items

### Words That Work vs Words to Avoid

| USE | NEVER USE |
|-----|-----------|
| "Limited run" | "SALE" / "DEAL" |
| "While stock lasts" | "HURRY!" |
| "Archive after sellout" | "Don't miss out!!!" |
| "First come, first served" | "Crazy discount" |
| "This colorway won't return" | "Unbeatable price" |

## THE DATA: DISCOUNT VS SCARCITY

| Strategy | Conversion Rate | Brand Impact |
|----------|----------------|--------------|
| No tactic | 1.5-3% | Neutral |
| 20% discount | 3-5% | Negative (lower LTV, higher returns) |
| Urgency/scarcity (no discount) | 2.5-4.5% | Positive (premium perception, higher LTV) |

**Urgency/scarcity achieves nearly the same conversion lift as discounting, with NONE of the downsides.**

---

## ACTION PLAN

1. **Hold at €45** for core hoodie. Never drop below.
2. **No discounts** except: 10% welcome email, free shipping over €75, one annual event (max 15%, 48hrs)
3. **Primary lever**: Limited drops (50-150 units), archive model, real stock counters
4. **TikTok content**: Weekly value comparisons, cost-per-wear, production breakdowns, post-drop stock updates
5. **Never compete on price**. Compete on weight, quality, community, and scarcity.
