**1. Engagement multiplier = slideshow ratio**

- @callme._emylii (5.3K followers) gets 170K avg views = 32x engagement

**3. Content style of top performers**
- **Moodboard aesthetic** -- curated, editorial, magazine-like slides

- **Consistent color palette** -- every slide feels like the same brand world

- **Trending audio** -- every single top performer uses trending sounds

**4. What's NOT working**

## THE 15 SLIDESHOW CONCEPTS FOR NEWGARMENTS

Each concept is designed to hit a specific conversion lever: **Trust**, **Desire**, **Scarcity**, or **Identity**. Use the Hook-Value-Action framework: Slide 1 = stop the scroll, Slides 2-5 = deliver value, Final slide = CTA.

---

### TRUST BUILDERS (These overcome "is this legit?")

---

#### SLIDESHOW 1: "What Your Money Actually Gets You"
**Goal:** Trust + Value justification
**Slides (6):**
1. **HOOK:** Close-up of hands holding the hoodie with text: "What does your money actually get you for EUR45?"
2. Fabric macro close-up -- text: "460 GSM French Terry. Not Gildan. Not a blank."
3. Inside-out shot showing double stitching -- text: "Double stitched at every stress point"
4. Side-by-side thickness comparison (your hoodie vs a fast fashion one) -- text: "Left: 180 GSM. Right: 460 GSM."
5. Packaging shot with handwritten note -- text: "Hand-packed. Personal note in every order."
6. **CTA:** Full piece flat lay -- text: "EUR45. Free worldwide shipping. No restock. Link in bio."
**Audio:** Trending lo-fi or chill instrumental
**Why it converts:** Directly answers the #1 objection. Every slide is a piece of evidence. The side-by-side on slide 4 is visual proof that can't be faked. High save rate because people will bookmark this as a "brand to check out."

---

#### SLIDESHOW 2: "Inside the Package"
**Goal:** Trust + Anticipation
**Slides (5):**
1. **HOOK:** Sealed NEWGARMENTS package on a clean surface -- text: "What a NEWGARMENTS order actually looks like"
2. Package being opened -- branded tissue paper visible
3. Hoodie being pulled out -- show the weight/drape as it unfolds
4. Close-up of the handwritten thank-you note
5. **CTA:** Full flat lay of everything laid out -- text: "Every order. Every time. Link in bio."
**Audio:** ASMR-style trending sound or soft beat
**Why it converts:** Unboxing content in slideshow form. The handwritten note is the emotional climax. People SAVE this to show friends or remember to order. This format consistently gets 500K+ views for small brands.

---

#### SLIDESHOW 3: "We're Not Hiding Anything"
**Goal:** Trust + Transparency (kills the "scam brand" fear)
**Slides (7):**
1. **HOOK:** Founder's hand holding up the product -- text: "We get called a scam brand. Fair. Let me show you everything."
2. Fabric swatch close-up with scale showing weight
3. Stitching macro shot -- text: "Double stitched. Reinforced stress points."
4. Screenshot of order notifications/dashboard (blur names) -- text: "Real orders. Real customers."
5. Photo of packaging station/workspace -- text: "Where every order gets packed"
6. Customer DM screenshot (with permission) saying something positive -- text: "Real feedback. Unedited."
7. **CTA:** Product shot -- text: "Judge for yourself. Link in bio."
**Audio:** A calm, confident trending sound
**Why it converts:** Names the objection directly in slide 1 -- this is pattern-interrupt gold. The audience's inner skeptic gets disarmed because YOU brought it up first. The evidence cascade (slides 2-6) overwhelms doubt. Very high share rate because people send this to friends saying "this brand seems legit."

### DESIRE BUILDERS (These make them WANT it)

---

### SCARCITY DRIVERS (These create urgency)

---

#### SLIDESHOW 8: "50 Made. Here's All of Them."
**Goal:** Scarcity + Proof
**Slides (5):**
1. **HOOK:** Overhead shot of all pieces stacked/laid out -- text: "We made 50. Here's all of them."
2. Close-up of the stack -- show the actual quantity
3. Individual piece -- detail shot
4. The piece being packed into the first order
5. **CTA:** The stack with a few missing -- text: "[X] claimed. When they're gone, they're archive."
**Audio:** Calm, factual trending sound
**Why it converts:** Real, visual scarcity. Not a fake countdown timer or "SELLING FAST" banner. The audience can literally COUNT the pieces. This is the opposite of what cheap brands do -- it's honest, calm, factual. "Archive" language resonates with your audience's love of rarity. @flexnsoul (93x engagement) does exactly this with drop teasers.

#### SLIDESHOW 9: "The Archive Wall"
**Goal:** Scarcity + FOMO + Brand equity
**Slides (6):**
1. **HOOK:** A wall/shelf/rack of past drops -- text: "Everything on this wall is sold out. Forever."
2. Close-up of past drop 1 -- text: "[Name] -- ARCHIVED"
3. Close-up of past drop 2 -- text: "[Name] -- ARCHIVED"
4. Close-up of past drop 3 -- text: "[Name] -- ARCHIVED"
5. Empty space on the wall/shelf -- text: "This spot is next."
6. **CTA:** New product shot -- text: "[NEW DROP NAME]. Dropping [date]. Don't let this one become a memory."
**Audio:** Atmospheric, slightly nostalgic trending sound
**Why it converts:** The archive wall is physical proof that your drops sell out and don't come back. Each "ARCHIVED" slide reinforces the no-restock policy. The empty spot on slide 5 is the power move -- it makes the NEW drop feel inevitable. This builds long-term brand equity: people save this to remember they need to act fast next time.

#### SLIDESHOW 10: "Countdown to Close"
**Goal:** Scarcity + Urgency (post this 24-48hrs before drop closes)
**Slides (4):**
1. **HOOK:** Product shot -- text: "This closes in [24/48] hours."
2. Stock remaining screenshot or number -- text: "[X] left in total. [Breakdown by size if possible]"
3. Customer wearing it / lifestyle shot -- text: "After this, it's archive."
4. **CTA:** Clean product shot -- text: "Last chance. Link in bio. No restock."
**Audio:** Trending sound with urgency/energy
**Why it converts:** Short, direct, urgent. No fluff. The size-specific stock info on slide 2 is crucial -- it makes scarcity personal ("my size is almost gone"). Post this at peak hours (7-10 PM) for maximum impact.

| Sunday | Rest or batch-create next week's content | |

**Total: 5-6 slideshows + 1-2 videos per week**

### The Rotation System

Don't repeat the same concept back-to-back. Rotate through the 4 categories:

**Week 1:** Trust > Desire > (video) > Identity > Scarcity > Engagement
**Week 2:** Desire > Trust > (video) > Scarcity > Identity > Engagement
**Week 3:** Identity > Scarcity > (video) > Trust > Desire > Engagement

This ensures every new follower sees proof, aspiration, urgency, AND identity content within their first week.

## PRODUCTION GUIDE

### Batch Content Creation (Do This Once Per Week, 60-90 Minutes)

**Setup:**
- Clean surface (white bedsheet, wooden table, or concrete)
- Window light (natural sidelight makes fabric texture pop)
- Phone on tripod or propped on books
- All pieces laid out and ready

**Batch 1: Product Shots (20 mins)**
- Flat lay overhead of each piece
- 45-degree angle showing fabric thickness
- Inside-out shot showing stitching
- Detail macro (fabric weave, label, print)
- Packaging flat lay (box, tissue paper, note)

**Batch 2: On-Body Shots (20 mins)**
- Full body front, side, back
- Walking shot (film 3 takes)
- Mirror fit check
- Detail shots while wearing (cuff, collar, hem)

**Batch 3: Comparison/Education Shots (20 mins)**
- Side-by-side with a cheap hoodie (thickness, stitching, label)
- Scale test photo
- Stack of multiple pieces
- Archive wall/shelf with past drops

**Assembly (20-30 mins):**
- Assemble slideshows in TikTok Photo Mode
- Add text overlays (one line per slide, bold clean font)
- Select trending audio for each
- Write captions with engagement hooks
- Schedule or post

### Image Quality Checklist
- [ ] Well-lit (natural light, no harsh shadows)
- [ ] Clean background (no clutter, consistent surface)
- [ ] Sharp focus (especially on detail/macro shots)
- [ ] Consistent color temperature across all slides
- [ ] 9:16 aspect ratio (1080x1920)
- [ ] Text is readable (not too small, not too much)

---

## TEXT OVERLAY RULES

1. **One line per slide. Maximum.**
2. **Bold, clean font.** No default TikTok font. Use bold serif or clean sans-serif.
3. **High contrast.** White text on dark images, dark text on light images. Always readable.
4. **Bottom third placement.** Keep text out of the center of the image -- let the product be the hero.
5. **Slide 1 text = the hook.** This is what stops the scroll. Make it curiosity-driven or emotionally charged.
6. **Final slide text = the CTA.** Always include what to do next: "Link in bio," "Comment your pick," "Follow for drop updates."

---

## AUDIO STRATEGY

- **ALWAYS add trending audio.** Even on photo carousels, the algorithm rewards trending sounds.
- Use TikTok's "trending" tab to find sounds gaining momentum
- Match the energy: calm/lo-fi for trust content, energetic/drill for desire content, atmospheric for scarcity content
- Don't use audio with lyrics that compete with your text overlays
- Save a playlist of 10-15 trending sounds weekly and rotate through them

## TRACKING & OPTIMIZATION

### KPIs to Track (In Order of Importance)
1. **Save rate** -- above 3% is good, above 5% is exceptional
2. **Swipe-through rate** -- are people reaching the final slide? Target 20%+
3. **Comment rate** -- above 0.5% is healthy
4. **Share rate** -- shares = algorithm gold
5. **Views** -- secondary metric; focus on ratios, not raw numbers
6. **Link clicks** (if using TikTok Shop or link in bio tracking)

### What to Do With the Data
- **High saves, low comments:** Content is valuable but not engaging. Add more questions/polls to text.
- **High comments, low saves:** Content is engaging but not valuable enough to bookmark. Add more educational/useful slides.
- **High shares, low everything else:** Content is entertaining/shocking but not converting. Add stronger CTAs.
- **Low swipe-through:** Slide 1 is working (they stop) but slides 2-3 are losing them. Make each slide reveal something new.
- **High everything on one type:** MAKE MORE OF IT. Double down on what works.
