# NEWGARMENTS — EASY CONTENT IDEAS
### No videographer needed. Just your phone, your pieces, and 10 minutes.

---

## FIRST: LEVEL UP YOUR FLAT LAYS (you're already doing these — make them hit harder)

Right now you're doing flat lays on the ground with text. That's actually a solid base. Here's how to make them 3x better without changing much:

### Surface matters
- **Stop using the ground.** Use a clean bed (white/grey sheets), a wooden table, or a plain concrete floor.
- Best cheap backgrounds: white bedsheet pulled tight, wooden desk, marble kitchen counter, black fabric draped flat
- One consistent surface for all your flat lays = instant brand cohesion

### Lighting
- Film near a window during daytime. Natural sidelight makes fabric texture pop.
- Never use overhead room light — it makes everything look flat and cheap
- Golden hour (1 hour before sunset near a window) = free cinematic lighting

### Angles
- Don't just shoot straight down. Try 45-degree angle — it shows fabric THICKNESS
- For hoodies: drape one sleeve off the edge of a table so it hangs. Shows weight.

### Text upgrades
- Stop using TikTok's default font. Use the bold serif or clean sans-serif options.
- Less text per screen. One line max. Let it breathe.
- Add specs as text: "460 GSM" "Double stitched" "Boxy fit" — one per cut

## 20 CONTENT IDEAS YOU CAN FILM ALONE IN YOUR ROOM

---

### IDEA 1: "Hand Lift" (5 seconds to film)
Hold the hoodie bunched up in one hand and just DROP it onto a table. The thud and the way it lands heavy shows the weight instantly. No talking needed.
**Text:** "When your hoodie actually has weight to it"
**Time to film:** 30 seconds

---

### IDEA 2: "The Flip" (show the inside)
Lay the piece flat. Grab the bottom hem and flip it inside out toward the camera in one smooth motion. Show the inner construction — fleece, stitching, clean seams.
**Text:** "The inside matters just as much"
**Time to film:** 1 minute

---

### IDEA 3: "Fabric Pinch" close-up
Zoom your phone camera in on the fabric. Pinch it between your fingers and pull. Show the thickness. Then do the same with a thin hoodie you own. Don't even label which is which — the difference is obvious.
**Text:** "Can your hoodie do this?"
**Time to film:** 2 minutes

---

### IDEA 4: "The Stack"
Stack ALL your pieces on top of each other. Build a tower. Then put your hand next to it for scale. A stack of 5 heavyweight hoodies should look massive.
**Text:** "5 hoodies. 4+ kilos of fabric."
**Time to film:** 2 minutes

---

### IDEA 5: "One Piece, Three Ways"
Lay out three different outfits using the SAME hoodie with different pants/accessories around it (flat lay style). Show versatility.
**Slide 1:** Hoodie + cargos + chunky sneakers
**Slide 2:** Hoodie + wide jeans + New Balance
**Slide 3:** Hoodie + joggers + AF1s
**Text per slide:** "1 hoodie. 3 fits. 0 misses."
**Time to film:** 5 minutes
**Platform:** Instagram carousel (gets massive saves)

---

### IDEA 6: "The Tag Shot"
Simple close-up of the brand tag/label inside the collar. Slowly pull it into focus.
**Text:** "Not Gildan. Not a blank. Built from scratch."
**Time to film:** 30 seconds
**Why:** Directly kills the #1 suspicion (is this just a printed blank?)

---

### IDEA 7: "Color Drop" series
Film one piece at a time. Black background (or dark bedsheet). Place the piece down gently from above — let it fall and settle. One piece per video. Post as a series.
**Text:** Just the piece name and "dropping [date]" or "available now"
**Time to film:** 1 minute per piece
**Why:** Simple, clean, repeatable. Builds anticipation across multiple posts.

---

### IDEA 8: "The Hanger Test"
Hang a NEWGARMENTS hoodie on a hanger. Then hang a thin hoodie next to it. The difference in how they drape and sit on the hanger is immediately visible. Heavyweight fabric hangs with structure. Thin fabric hangs limp.
**Text:** "Left: fast fashion. Right: NEWGARMENTS. See the difference."
**Time to film:** 1 minute

---

### IDEA 9: "Unbox Your Own Product"
This sounds weird but it works. Pack up one of your orders — full branded packaging, tissue paper, handwritten note — then film yourself unboxing it. Pretend you're the customer. Show the full experience.
**Text:** "What every NEWGARMENTS order looks like when it arrives"
**Time to film:** 5 minutes
**Why:** People need to see the experience before they trust you with their money

---

### IDEA 10: "Spec Slide" carousel
Make a carousel where each slide is ONE close-up detail shot with ONE spec written on it.
- Slide 1: Full piece flat lay — "THE [NAME]"
- Slide 2: Fabric close-up — "460 GSM FRENCH TERRY"
- Slide 3: Stitching close-up — "DOUBLE STITCHED SEAMS"
- Slide 4: Cuff close-up — "RIBBED CUFFS"
- Slide 5: Label close-up — "DESIGNED IN THE NETHERLANDS"
- Slide 6: Piece on body or styled flat lay — "€[PRICE]. NO RESTOCK."
**Time to film:** 10 minutes (just close-ups of different parts)
**Why:** Carousels get 2-3x more saves on Instagram. Saves = more reach. This is your product page in carousel form.

---

### IDEA 11: "The Throw"
Toss the hoodie toward the camera from a few feet away. Catch it right before it covers the lens. The weight of it flying through the air looks and sounds different from thin fabric.
**Text:** "460 GSM hitting different"
**Time to film:** 2 minutes (might need a few takes)

---

### IDEA 12: "Packing ASMR" (phone propped up, overhead)
Prop your phone facing down (use a stack of books or a phone mount). Film yourself packing an order from above:
1. Fold the hoodie
2. Wrap in tissue paper
3. Write the handwritten note
4. Place everything in the packaging
5. Seal it
No talking. No music. Just the sounds of fabric, paper, and pen.
**Time to film:** 5 minutes
**Why:** This format gets 500K+ views regularly for small brands. The handwritten note is the moment people share.

---

### IDEA 13: "POV: You Open Your NEWGARMENTS Order"
Film from your perspective (holding phone in one hand) as you open the package with the other. Show everything: the packaging, the tissue paper, the note, pulling the hoodie out and feeling the weight.
**Text:** "When it's heavier than you expected >>>>"
**Time to film:** 3 minutes

---

### IDEA 14: "Comment Response" format
Screenshot a comment or DM that asks "is this legit?" or "what's the quality like?" — then film a video responding to it by showing the actual product.
**Hook:** "Someone asked if NEWGARMENTS is actually worth it. Let me show you."
Then just show the piece — flip it, show the stitching, show the weight, show the packaging.
**Time to film:** 3 minutes
**Why:** This format is native to TikTok. It feels like a conversation, not an ad. And it directly addresses the skepticism your audience has.
**Pro tip:** If no one has commented yet, ask a friend to comment or DM you the question. Or just use the text: "Most asked question in our DMs"

---

### IDEA 15: "Mirror Fit Check" (if you wear your own stuff)
Stand in front of a mirror. Phone in hand. Show the fit from different angles. No talking needed — just let the piece speak.
**Text:** "[Piece name] / [GSM] / [Fit type] / [Price]"
**Time to film:** 2 minutes
**Why:** Fit checks are the #1 most consistent streetwear format on TikTok. And if the founder wears the brand, it instantly builds credibility.

---

### IDEA 16: "What [Price] Gets You"
Flat lay showing everything that comes with the order: the piece, the packaging, the note, any extras.
**Text:** "What €45 gets you from NEWGARMENTS"
Then in a second clip or next slide: show a sad, thin hoodie in a plastic mailer bag
**Text:** "What €45 gets you from a TikTok brand"
**Time to film:** 5 minutes

---

### IDEA 17: "Detail You Missed"
Zoom into one specific detail that people might not notice — the way the seam is finished, a hidden label, embroidery texture, the thickness of the drawstring cord.
**Text:** "Details most brands skip"
**Time to film:** 1 minute per video
**Why:** This can be a recurring series. One detail per post. Shows you care about the small things.

---

### IDEA 18: "Day vs Night" flat lay
Two flat lays side by side:
- Left: daytime outfit (lighter styling, casual vibe)
- Right: night outfit (darker, layered, going-out vibe)
Same hoodie in both.
**Text:** "One piece. Day to night."
**Time to film:** 5 minutes

---

### IDEA 19: "Rate This Fit" engagement bait
Post a flat lay outfit combination. Caption or text: "Rate this fit 1-10"
That's it. Don't overthink it. The engagement comes from people commenting their rating. Comments = algorithmic boost = more reach.
**Time to film:** 2 minutes
**Post as:** TikTok, Instagram Reel, or feed post

---

### IDEA 20: "The Collection" overhead reveal
Lay a dark cloth or paper over all your pieces. Slowly pull it away to reveal the full collection laid out underneath.
**Text:** "Dropping [date]. No restock."
**Audio:** A beat drop timed to the reveal moment
**Time to film:** 3 minutes

## EQUIPMENT YOU NEED (nothing expensive)

- **Your phone.** That's the camera. Newer iPhones or Samsungs shoot better than most cameras for social content.
- **A phone tripod or stack of books.** For overhead shots and stable flat lays.
- **A window.** Natural light is free and better than any ring light for fabric texture.
- **One clean surface.** White bedsheet, wooden table, or clean concrete.
- **One background for videos.** A plain wall, a clean corner of your room, or even a hung-up bedsheet.

**Optional but helpful:**
- A cheap clip-on macro lens (€10-15 on Amazon) for extreme close-ups of fabric and stitching
- A basic ring light for filming at night (€15-25)
- A kitchen scale for weight comparison videos (€10)

## THE GOLDEN RULES

1. **Raw > polished.** Your audience trusts shaky phone footage more than cinematic ads. Don't overthink production.
2. **Film in batches.** Spend 30-60 minutes once a week filming 5-7 clips. Edit and post throughout the week.
3. **Every video should show weight or quality.** Even in a flat lay — drape a sleeve off the edge to show thickness. In every video, the fabric should look HEAVY.
4. **One message per video.** Don't try to say everything in one post. One video = one point. "This is heavyweight." "This doesn't restock." "This is what you get for €45."
5. **Post consistently, not perfectly.** 5 decent TikToks per week beats 1 "perfect" one. The algorithm rewards consistency.
6. **Steal formats, not ideas.** Watch what Corteiz, Cole Buxton, Broken Planet, and Scuffers post. Copy the FORMAT (flat lay reveal, packing ASMR, fit check) but make it yours.
7. **The hook is everything.** If the first frame doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters. Always ask: "Would I stop scrolling for this?"
