## 1. PLATFORM OVERVIEW & ALGORITHM MECHANICS

### Two Types of TikTok Slideshows

**Swipeable Slideshows (Photo Mode / Carousel)**
- Viewers manually tap/swipe through images at their own pace
- Supports up to 35 photos per post
- Best for: product catalogs, lookbooks, step-by-step content, outfit breakdowns
- Treated DIFFERENTLY by TikTok's algorithm than video — has its own distribution logic

**Non-Swipeable Slideshows**
- Auto-play like traditional videos with background music
- Better for: brand storytelling, moodboards, announcements, behind-the-scenes
- Behaves more like standard video in the algorithm

### How TikTok's Algorithm Ranks Carousels (2026)

TikTok does NOT treat carousels the same way it treats videos. It uses different signals:

**Primary Ranking Signals:**

| Signal | What It Means | Target Benchmark |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| Swipe-Through Rate | % of viewers who swipe past slide 1 | Above 60% |
| Dwell Time Per Slide | Time spent on each slide before advancing | 3-5 seconds optimal |
| Reverse Swipes | Users swiping backward to revisit slides | More = better; include stats/reference material to encourage this |
| Completion Rate | % of viewers who reach the final slide | 40-60% for 8+ slide carousels |

**Secondary Engagement Signals:**
- **Saves/Bookmarks**: Weighted EXTREMELY heavily for carousels — the single most important secondary metric
- **Shares**: Strong signal that content is worth spreading
- **Comments**: Helpful but weighted less than saves/shares for carousel format
- **New Follows**: Indicates audience expansion

**Distribution Process:**
1. Initial test push to 200-500 people (mostly followers + niche enthusiasts)
2. Algorithm analyzes engagement signals during this test phase
3. Strong performance triggers wider distribution in waves
4. Each successive wave can reach exponentially larger audiences

**Key Insight**: TikTok has been ACTIVELY promoting carousel content since late 2024. Carousels that hit strong engagement signals can reach just as many people as videos. Educational and list-based carousels often OUTPERFORM videos in saves and shares.

### Technical Specifications

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| Aspect Ratio | 9:16 (portrait) recommended; 1:1 and 16:9 also supported |
| Resolution | 1080 x 1920 pixels minimum |
| File Types | JPG, PNG |
| Max File Size | Under 20MB per image |
| Max Photos | 35 per slideshow (Photo Mode); 15 via templates |
| Font Minimum | 36pt for mobile readability |
| Caption Limit | 2,200 characters |

## 3. VIRAL SLIDESHOW FORMATS & TRENDS

### TREND 1: "Outfits of the Week" / Weekly Fits Slideshow

**Description**: Creator showcases 5-7 different outfits (Monday through Friday/Sunday), one per slide, photographed in the same location or with consistent aesthetic.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slide 1: Hook text ("my fits this week" / "weekly rotation")
- Slides 2-6: Full-body outfit photos, one per day, with day label as text overlay
- Slide 7: CTA ("which day wins?" / "follow for more fits")

**Example brands using it:** Aelfric Eden (posts Monday-Friday fits every week), various streetwear creators

**Engagement pattern:** High comment engagement as viewers vote for their favorite day. High save rate as users bookmark for outfit inspiration.

**When it became popular:** Evolved from Instagram OOTD culture; surged on TikTok mid-2024, remains strong through 2026

**Text overlay patterns:** Day labels (MON / TUE / WED), brand tags, "which is your fave?"

**Music/audio:** Trending lo-fi beats, sped-up pop songs, "Aperture" audio trending in early 2026

**Why it works:** Creates natural engagement hook (voting), showcases product range, easy to produce, encourages daily checking-in

**How to replicate for streetwear:** Photograph 5-7 complete streetwear looks in consistent urban setting. Use bold sans-serif font for day labels. End with engagement question. Post every Monday.

---

### TREND 2: The "Fit Check" Carousel

**Description**: Single outfit broken down across multiple slides showing the full look, close-up details, different angles, and item identification/pricing.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slide 1: Full-body shot with "fit check" or "rate this fit" text
- Slide 2: Close-up of top/jacket with brand tag
- Slide 3: Close-up of pants with brand tag
- Slide 4: Close-up of shoes/accessories
- Slide 5: Back view or action shot
- Slide 6: Item breakdown with prices

**Engagement pattern:** High save rate (users save for shopping reference), strong comment engagement (rating requests)

**When it became popular:** Ongoing since 2023, evolved with Photo Mode capabilities

**Text overlay patterns:** "Rate 1-10", brand names on each piece, price tags, "link in bio"

**Music/audio:** Trending audio from fit check hashtag, often bass-heavy beats

**Why it works:** Satisfies curiosity about specific items, creates natural save behavior, drives product discovery

**How to replicate:** Photograph one standout streetwear look from 4-5 angles. Label each piece with brand + price. Ask for ratings.

---

### TREND 3: Before/After Transformation Slideshow

**Description**: Two contrasting slides showing a dramatic visual transformation — applied to fashion as style evolutions, glow-ups, or seasonal wardrobe transitions.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slide 1: "Before" state (e.g., basic outfit, old style, bundled winter look)
- Slide 2: "After" reveal (elevated streetwear look, summer era, new collection)
- Can extend to multi-slide progression showing evolution over time

**Example format:** "slide one is you bundled up in winter, slide two is your summer era in full effect"

**Engagement pattern:** High share rate, strong completion rate due to curiosity gap

**When it became popular:** Evolved from broader transformation content; surged with carousel format late 2024-2025

**Text overlay patterns:** "Then vs Now", year labels, "style evolution"

**Music/audio:** Songs with dramatic builds or beat drops; Lil Nas X "That's What I Want" was trending for flashback content in early 2026

**Why it works:** Curiosity gap drives swipe-through; emotional resonance; highly shareable

**How to replicate:** Create contrast between "basic" and "styled" looks using your brand's pieces. Show the transformation your brand enables.

---

### TREND 4: Photo Dump / Aesthetic Lifestyle Carousel

**Description**: Casual, seemingly unplanned collection of 5-10 photos that capture a vibe or aesthetic moment — outfits interspersed with lifestyle shots.

**What the slides look like:**
- Mix of outfit photos, food, scenery, candid moments, detail shots
- Feels curated but not overly polished
- "Aperture" has become a trending backdrop for photo dumps in early 2026

**Engagement pattern:** High saves and shares, feels authentic and relatable

**When it became popular:** Migrated from Instagram; TikTok adopted aggressively from 2024. Major surge in 2025 with Photo Mode improvements.

**Text overlay patterns:** Minimal or none — the aesthetic speaks for itself. Sometimes a single word or date stamp.

**Music/audio:** Lo-fi, indie, chill tracks. Currently trending with dreamy/atmospheric sounds.

**Why it works:** Moves away from overly polished content; feels authentic; captures the "belonging" feeling; TikTok's cinematic pacing adds dynamic energy vs Instagram's static feel

**How to replicate:** Mix 3-4 outfit shots with lifestyle images (coffee, skyline, sneaker close-up, friend group). Keep it casual. Use cohesive color grading. Let the vibe sell the brand.

### TREND 5: Brand Tier List / Ranking Slideshow

**Description**: Creator ranks streetwear brands in tier list format (S through F tier), generating massive debate in comments.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slide 1: "Ranking streetwear brands" with tier list graphic
- Slides 2-7: Each tier with brand logos and brief justification
- OR: Single comprehensive tier list image followed by explanation slides

**Popular tier rankings (2025-2026):**
- S-Tier brands frequently included: Corteiz, Stussy, Sp5der, ADWYSD
- Frequently debated: Supreme, Off-White, Trapstar, Stone Island, BAPE

**Engagement pattern:** EXTREMELY high comment rate (debate-driven), high share rate, strong replay value

**When it became popular:** Tier list format has been ongoing; streetwear-specific version surged 2024-2025

**Text overlay patterns:** Tier labels (S/A/B/C/D/F), brand names, "agree or disagree?"

**Music/audio:** Often conversational/commentary audio or trending beats

**Why it works:** Controversy and opinion-based content drives comments; viewers feel compelled to share their own ranking; creates community engagement

**How to replicate:** Create a tier list that strategically places your brand favorably but includes enough controversial opinions to spark debate. Or create "rating my collection" content.

### TREND 6: "Rating My Fits Over the Years" Slideshow

**Description**: Creator showcases outfits from different time periods, rating each one, showing style evolution.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slides progress chronologically (2019 -> 2020 -> 2021... -> 2026)
- Each slide has the outfit photo + year + self-rating (usually /10)
- Often shows progression from "cringe" to current style

**Engagement pattern:** High completion rate (viewers want to see the full evolution), strong comment engagement

**When it became popular:** Has been a recurring format; template available on TikTok

**Text overlay patterns:** Year labels, ratings out of 10, self-deprecating commentary on earlier fits

**Why it works:** Nostalgia + relatability + transformation narrative

**How to replicate:** Show your brand's evolution or a customer's style journey featuring your pieces. Can also be used as "rating our past collections."

---

### TREND 7: "This or That" Fashion Slideshow

**Description**: Presents two outfit/styling options per slide, asking viewers to choose their preference.

**What the slides look like:**
- Each slide split into two options (side by side or sequential)
- Labels: "Option A" vs "Option B" or "This" vs "That"
- Often themed (back-to-school, date night, casual vs dressy)

**Engagement pattern:** High comment rate (voting), high replay rate, strong shares

**Text overlay patterns:** "This or That?", "Pick one!", option labels

**Music/audio:** Upbeat trending sounds

**Why it works:** Gamification of fashion; low barrier to engage (just comment a letter); creates community

**How to replicate:** Style the same pieces two ways, or show two complete looks. Ask audience to vote. Use as product launch content (new colorway A vs B).

---

### TREND 8: "Day in the Life" Outfit Slideshow

**Description**: Documents different outfits worn throughout a single day or across a week, mixed with lifestyle context shots.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slide 1: Morning outfit (casual/commute)
- Slide 2: Work/school look
- Slide 3: Gym/athletic wear
- Slide 4: Evening/going-out fit
- Interspersed with lifestyle moments (coffee, transit, activities)

**Engagement pattern:** High save rate (aspirational content), moderate-high shares

**When it became popular:** Evolved from YouTube "day in my life" vlogs; slideshow format surged 2025

**Text overlay patterns:** Time stamps, location names, minimal captions

**Music/audio:** "Day in the life" trending audios, often lo-fi or energetic depending on vibe

**Why it works:** Shows versatility of wardrobe; creates aspirational lifestyle association; natural product placement

**How to replicate:** Document a full day wearing your brand's pieces in different contexts. Show the brand fitting into real life.

### TREND 9: Product Showcase / New Drop Teaser Slideshow

**Description**: Brand uses carousel to build hype for upcoming collection or showcase new products.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slide 1: Teaser image with "New drop" or date announcement
- Slides 2-5: Individual product shots (flat lays, on-model, detail shots)
- Slide 6: Collection overview
- Final slide: CTA ("New drop now live" / "Link in bio" / "Comment LINK")

**Example:** UK streetwear brand 8azmo used TikTok slideshows to showcase vintage football apparel, resonating with target audience.

**Engagement pattern:** High save rate (bookmark for drop day), moderate-high shares

**Text overlay patterns:** Drop date, "coming soon", price points, "limited edition", "tap link in bio"

**Music/audio:** Hype beats, bass-heavy instrumentals, sometimes brand-original audio

**Why it works:** Builds anticipation; creates FOMO; saves serve as reminders; still images let viewers examine product details

**How to replicate:** Photograph new collection pieces individually + styled. Lead with strongest piece. Include date + scarcity messaging. End with clear CTA.

### TREND 10: Haul Slideshow

**Description**: Shows multiple items from a shopping haul, one per slide, with try-on or flat-lay images.

**What the slides look like:**
- Slide 1: All items together or shopping bag shot
- Slides 2-8: Individual items, either flat-lay or try-on
- Each slide has item name + price + where to buy
- Final slide: Full outfit put together from haul items

**Historical context:** #sheinhaul reached 4.8 billion views; Amazon hauls at 968 million; ASOS at 131 million

**Engagement pattern:** High save rate, strong comment engagement ("where did you get X?")

**Popular brands featured:** Shein, Amazon, PacSun, Lululemon, Free People, Brandy Melville, Edikted

**Text overlay patterns:** Item name, price, store name, "TikTok made me buy it"

**Music/audio:** Upbeat pop or trending sounds

**Why it works:** Product discovery format; satisfies shopping curiosity; creates aspirational purchasing lists

**How to replicate:** Create "unboxing" or "new in" slideshows for your streetwear drops. Show each piece individually then styled together.

### TREND 11: Outfit Transition / Swipe Slideshow

**Description**: Creator films outfit changes using swipe motion transitions, compiled into a carousel that looks like clothes are magically swapping.

**What the slides look like:**
- Each slide shows a different outfit in the exact same position/pose
- Created by filming swipe motions, splitting clips in CapCut, screenshotting each outfit
- Background removed from outfit screenshots, used as overlays with keyframe animations

**How it's made (technical):**
1. Film swipe motions in the exact same spot wearing different outfits
2. Add all clips into CapCut
3. Split each clip at the swipe and delete extra parts
4. Screenshot each outfit
5. Add screenshots as overlays with background removed (only the clothes)
6. Use keyframes to swipe outfits in and out

**Engagement pattern:** High view counts due to visual appeal, moderate-high shares

**When it became popular:** Mid-2024, remains popular through 2026

**Music/audio:** Beat-driven songs that sync with transitions

**Why it works:** Visually satisfying; showcases product variety; feels creative and polished

**How to replicate:** Film model in same spot wearing 5-7 different looks from your brand. Edit using CapCut Clone effect with mask match cuts and keyframes.

### TREND 12: "Thrift or Pass" Slideshow

**Description**: Creator shows thrifted items one by one, asking audience to vote "thrift" (keep) or "pass" (return).

**What the slides look like:**
- Each slide shows one thrifted item (flat-lay or try-on)
- Text overlay: "Thrift or Pass?"
- Mix of trendy finds and vintage gems

**Engagement pattern:** Very high comment rate (voting mechanism)

**Why it works:** Gamification; sustainability angle; creates community decision-making

**How to replicate:** Adapt as "Cop or Drop" for new streetwear pieces. Show items and let community vote.

### TREND 13: Storytelling / Journey Carousel

**Description**: Multi-slide narrative that takes viewers through a process, journey, or behind-the-scenes story.

**What the slides look like:**
- Structured like intro -> context -> development -> reveal -> CTA
- For brands: design process, production journey, inspiration board -> final product

**Example:** Youthforia's Shark Tank carousel achieved 6.6 million views by sharing founder insights with emotional resonance.

**Engagement pattern:** High saves (story worth revisiting), strong shares

**Why it works:** Emotional connection; deeper relationship building through narrative depth; works exceptionally well for small brands showing "the messy middle"

**How to replicate:** Document your brand's story: design sketches -> fabric selection -> sampling -> final product. Show the human side of your streetwear brand.

## 5. TEXT OVERLAY PATTERNS

### Best Practices

| Guideline | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Words per slide | Maximum 15-20 words |
| Font size | Minimum 24pt (36pt recommended for mobile) |
| Contrast | High contrast colors required |
| Format | Bullet points or numbered lists preferred |
| Style | Simple fonts that don't distract from images |
| Position | Don't cover focal points of the image |

### Proven Hook Formulas for Slide 1

- "The [number] thing nobody tells you about [topic]"
- "Stop doing [common mistake]"
- Surprising statistics or counterintuitive claims
- Audience-relevant questions
- "5 secrets experts don't want you to know..."
- "[Brand/item] but make it [aesthetic]"
- "POV: you finally found [item type]"
- "Rating my fits from [year] to [year]"

### CTA Patterns for Final Slide

- "Save this for later"
- "Tap the link in bio"
- "Comment your fave"
- "New drop now live"
- "Send this to someone who needs this"
- "Follow for more fits"
- "Which one wins?"

### Engagement-Driving Text Patterns

- "Send this to..." prompts (drives shares)
- Numbered indicators ("1/8") to signal more content
- Rating scales ("Rate this fit 1-10")
- Voting prompts ("This or That?")
- Price tags on each item (drives saves)

## 6. MUSIC & AUDIO PATTERNS

### General Guidelines

- **Use TikTok's Commercial Music Library** to avoid copyright issues (wrong track = video muted, taken down, or DMCA notice)
- Check TikTok's "Trending" section in the sounds library
- Using trending audio can reportedly 10x your reach as content appears in that sound's feed
- Adding a trending sound gives a significant algorithm visibility boost

### Currently Trending Audio Types for Fashion Content

| Audio Type | When to Use | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Sped-up pop cuts | Energetic outfit showcases, transitions | Trending mainstream songs at 1.25-1.5x speed |
| Lo-fi beats | Aesthetic photo dumps, lifestyle carousels | Chill hip-hop instrumentals |
| Bass-heavy instrumentals | Streetwear drop teasers, fit checks | Trap/drill beats |
| "That's my life" bridge (sped-up) | Vlog-style highlight reels under 20 seconds | Life recap slideshows |
| Lil Nas X "That's What I Want" | Flashback/transformation content | Past vs present slideshows |
| "Aperture" audio | Photo dumps, relationship/friend milestones | Lifestyle carousels |
| Commentary/conversational audio | Tier lists, opinion content | Voice-over ranking content |

### Audio Strategy

- Match audio energy to content mood (chill for aesthetic, energetic for hype)
- Beat drops can sync with slide transitions for non-swipeable slideshows
- Trending sounds have a lifecycle — catch them early for maximum reach
- Brand-original audio can build identity (e.g., Boohoo's "It's from Boohoo" original song)

## 8. HOW TO REPLICATE FOR A STREETWEAR BRAND

### Recommended Content Calendar (Weekly)

| Day | Content Type | Format |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Monday | "Weekly Fits" carousel | 5-7 slides, one outfit per day |
| Tuesday | Product spotlight / Fit Check | 5-6 slides, detail breakdown |
| Wednesday | Behind-the-scenes / Brand story | 7-10 slides, narrative arc |
| Thursday | "This or That" / Community poll | 4-6 slides, voting format |
| Friday | New drop teaser / Product launch | 5-8 slides, hype content |
| Saturday | Photo dump / Lifestyle aesthetic | 8-12 slides, mixed content |
| Sunday | Tier list / Rating content | 5-10 slides, opinion-driven |

### Production Checklist

1. **Photography Setup**
   - Consistent shooting location (urban backdrop, clean wall, studio)
   - Natural lighting or ring light
   - Full-body and detail shots for each piece
   - Minimum 1080x1920px resolution (9:16)
   - JPG or PNG format, under 20MB per image

2. **Slide Structure**
   - Slide 1: HOOK — bold visual + intriguing text (spend 2-3 seconds)
   - Slides 2-3: Main content (3-4 seconds each)
   - Middle slides: Value delivery, product details
   - Final slide: Clear CTA (4-5 seconds display time)

3. **Text Overlay Rules**
   - Max 15-20 words per slide
   - 36pt font minimum
   - High-contrast colors
   - Don't cover the product
   - Use numbered indicators (1/8, 2/8...)

4. **Audio Selection**
   - Check TikTok Trending sounds weekly
   - Use Commercial Music Library only
   - Match energy to content type
   - Consider creating brand-original audio

5. **Caption & Hashtag Strategy**
   - Caption: 200+ characters with keywords for TikTok SEO
   - 3-5 trending hashtags (discovery)
   - 2-3 niche hashtags (#streetwear, #fitcheck, #mensfashion)
   - 1-2 branded hashtags

6. **Posting Optimization**
   - Post during 6-10 PM in target audience timezone
   - 3-5 carousels per week minimum
   - Respond to comments within 2 hours (boosts engagement velocity)
