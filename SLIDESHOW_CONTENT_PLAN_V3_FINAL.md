## THE 7 PROVEN FIRST-SLIDE STRATEGIES (Ranked by Performance Data)

Before the concepts -- these are the slide 1 formulas that are proven to work. Every concept below uses one of these.

### 1. BOLD SPECIFIC CLAIM (Highest CTR)
**What:** A hyper-specific statement that makes a promise or reveals something unexpected.
**Example:** "The EUR45 hoodie that weighs more than Cole Buxton's EUR130 one"
**Why it works:** TikTok Creative Center data shows hooks with specific claims in the first 3 seconds decrease CPA by 16%. Vague hooks ("Nice streetwear") get scrolled past. Specific hooks ("460 GSM for EUR45") stop the thumb.
**Visual:** Bold white text on a moody product photo or dark background. The text IS the hook. Image supports it.
**Used by:** Represent CLO (10,303 clicks), e.l.f. Beauty (5B+ hashtag views)

### 2. PERSON/FACE ON SLIDE 1 (Highest Scroll-Stop Rate)
**What:** A real person wearing the piece. Face visible, candid pose, natural setting.
**Why it works:** Human faces are the #1 scroll-stopper on TikTok. Creator @brock11johnson (carousel specialist) specifically recommends putting a cutout or person on slide 1. Broken Planet's entire viral strategy is street-level photography of REAL people, not product shots.
**Visual:** Person in your product, shot from slightly below (power angle), urban setting, natural light. NOT a studio model pose -- candid, raw, like a friend took the photo.
**Used by:** Broken Planet (1.3M views/24hrs), SKIMS (100M+ views), Gymshark (110M+ views)

### 3. BEFORE/AFTER CURIOSITY GAP (Highest Swipe-Through Rate)
**What:** Two contrasting images or states side-by-side on slide 1 that make you NEED to see the rest.
**Example:** A basic/boring outfit next to a fire fit with text "Same person. Same budget."
**Why it works:** Creates cognitive tension that can only be resolved by swiping. The brain cannot leave a comparison unfinished.
**Visual:** Split frame: left side = "before" (dim, flat), right side = "after" (vibrant, confident). Or: cheap hoodie vs heavyweight hoodie side-by-side.
**Used by:** Vitamasques (doubled CTR with before/after carousels)

### 4. INTERACTIVE QUESTION (Highest Comment Rate)
**What:** A question that demands a response. "Which one?" "Pick one." "A, B, or C?"
**Why it works:** Comments are the second-most important algorithm signal. A question forces engagement. People can't just passively swipe -- they have to CHOOSE.
**Visual:** Multiple products/colors/options laid out. Each clearly labeled A, B, C or with color names. Clean, overhead shot.
**Used by:** Top-performing fashion carousels across TikTok (official Creative Center recommendation)

### 5. UNEXPECTED/BIZARRE IMAGE (Highest Share Rate)
**What:** Something visually jarring or unexpected that breaks the pattern of what people expect to see in their feed.
**Example:** A hoodie on a kitchen scale. Scissors cutting into a hoodie. A pile of 50 hoodies stacked to absurd height.
**Why it works:** Pattern interrupts trigger the "what is this?" reflex. People share bizarre content more than any other type.
**Visual:** Unusual angle, unexpected object placement, or product in a context where you wouldn't normally see it.
**Used by:** Burberry (14.6M views on a lo-fi office printer video -- completely unexpected for a luxury brand)

### 6. STREET-LEVEL RAW PHOTOGRAPHY (Highest Authenticity Signal)
**What:** A real, unpolished photo that looks like someone's friend took it on the street. NOT a product photo. NOT a studio shot.
**Visual:** Grainy, slightly dark, natural street setting, candid angle. Think "screenshot from a cool person's Instagram story."
**Why it works:** Broken Planet's entire strategy is built on this. Their content feels "native" to the platform -- not like an ad. TikTok's own data shows "raw, messy, and real" outperforms polished content. The algorithm rewards content that looks like it was made by a creator, not a brand.
**Used by:** Broken Planet (sold out in 40 minutes), @something.archive (97% slideshow, 4x engagement), @god._.hand (100% slideshow, 114K followers)

### 7. TEXT-ONLY BOLD STATEMENT (Highest Curiosity)
**What:** Black slide, bold white text, nothing else. A statement so strong it doesn't need an image.
**Example:** "I threw away the first 4 versions of this hoodie."
**Why it works:** In a sea of images and videos, a text-only slide stands out by being radically different. It also triggers the Zeigarnik effect -- an incomplete statement demands resolution (swiping to the next slide).
**Visual:** Pure black or very dark background. White or off-white bold sans-serif text (Inter, Montserrat). Centered. One sentence max.
**Used by:** HUH Clothing (2M views on emotional storytelling format)
